# Research Section Format & Citation Rules

This document details the editorial guidelines, structure, and academic citation rules for publications in the Research section.

---

## 1. Section Purpose & Mandate
The [research.html](file:///Users/aravindsaj/Library/CloudStorage/GoogleDrive-ask5615@g.rit.edu/My%20Drive/DeskEssentials/Systems/Insight%20Website/research.html) page hosts technical whitepapers, logistic research, and e-commerce distribution insights. The objective is to establish Aoura Insights as an industry thought-leader in GCC commerce infrastructure.

---

## 2. Research Report Structure
Every research report must follow a strict, logical layout format:
- **Title**: Analytical and descriptive (e.g., *"Optimizing Middle-Mile Logistics in the GCC: A Spatial Analysis"*).
- **Abstract / Executive Summary**: A bolded introductory section summarizing findings in under 150 words.
- **Introduction**: Problem statement, logistics context, and scope of study.
- **Methodology**: Technical breakdown of data gathering, telemetry, or shipping models.
- **Analysis & Findings**: Data presentation using monochrome tables and minimal visual graphs.
- **Operational Implications**: Practical translation of findings for Aoura Products or Aoura E-commerce Services.
- **Conclusion**: A summary of key takeaways and strategic recommendations.

---

## 3. Citation & Referencing Rules
To ensure "intelligence-grade" credibility, all external statistics or studies must be explicitly cited:
- **In-text Citations**: Use bracketed numerical indices corresponding to references (e.g., `[1]`, `[2]`).
- **Data Source Standards**: Cite reputable entities (e.g., Dubai Chamber of Commerce, GCC Statistical Center, McKinsey, Gartner).
- **Bibliography Format**: Place a `"References"` list at the foot of each article using clean APA-style layouts:
  ```text
  [1] Author Lastname, F. M. (Year). Title of Report. Publisher / Source Name. URL (if applicable).
  ```
